What Is Medical Thermography Equipment, Exactly?

Medical thermography equipment is a category of infrared imaging hardware and software used to visualize surface temperature patterns for clinical assessment.

It combines a medical-grade infrared camera (a sensor that captures heat radiation in the 7-14 micrometer range) with reporting software designed for clinical documentation and HIPAA compliance.

Unlike industrial thermal cameras used in local service or electrical work, medical-grade systems must meet stricter resolution, calibration, and data-handling standards. The American Academy of Thermology (a professional body founded in 1972 — aathermology.org) sets practice guidelines that influence which equipment is considered clinically defensible. Infrared thermography, digital infrared thermal imaging (DITI), and clinical thermal imaging are all terms used interchangeably for this modality.

What Credentials Should a Thermography Vendor Have?

Vendor credentials are the verifiable signals that a thermography supplier meets safety, privacy, and clinical standards.

Legitimate vendors carry FDA registration, HIPAA Business Associate Agreement capability, and alignment with established thermology guidelines.

  • FDA Establishment Registration under 21 CFR Part 807 — verify on the FDA registration database.
  • Business Associate Agreement (BAA) capability — required under HIPAA's Privacy Rule (45 CFR §164.502(e)) for any vendor handling PHI.
  • Alignment with American Academy of Thermology guidelines (certified by AAT — aathermology.org).
  • UL or IEC 60601-1 electrical safety certification for medical devices.
  • SOC 2 Type II attestation for SaaS components such as TotalVision.

What Are the Myths About Thermography Equipment?

Several persistent myths drive bad purchasing decisions; the facts come from FDA guidance and clinical standards.

Myth: Any thermal camera works for medical use.

Fact: Only FDA-registered, medical-grade cameras meeting 21 CFR Part 807 are appropriate for clinical reporting.

Myth: HIPAA doesn't apply to image files stored locally.

Fact: 45 CFR §164.312 applies to all electronic PHI regardless of storage location.

Myth: Thermography isn't reimbursable.

Fact: CPT codes including 93740 and 93799 support documentation, though coverage varies by payer.

Myth: Higher resolution always means better clinical results.

Fact: Sensor sensitivity (NETD) matters more than raw pixel count above 320×240.

#Red flags to watch for

  • Vendor refuses to provide a Business Associate Agreement.
  • No FDA establishment registration listed on the public FDA database.
  • Demands full payment upfront with no financing alternative.
  • Software has no audit log or user-access controls.
  • No published list of supported CPT codes or reporting templates.
  • Support hours limited to a single time zone with no after-hours coverage.
