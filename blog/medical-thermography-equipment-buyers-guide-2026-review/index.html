What Makes a Thermography Camera Medical Grade?

A medical-grade thermography camera is an infrared imager calibrated and specified for clinical thermal patterning rather than industrial or building inspection use. The threshold specs are tighter, the calibration documentation more rigorous, and FDA listing is required for U.S. clinical sale.

Medical-grade thermography cameras need NETD under 0.05°C, FDA 510(k) clearance pathway documentation, and clinical-protocol calibration.

The key specifications buyers should verify:

  1. Thermal sensitivity (NETD): Under 0.05°C — preferably 0.03°C for breast and neuromuscular work.
  2. Resolution: Minimum 320×240 pixels; 640×480 for whole-body or breast imaging.
  3. Spectral range: 7.5–14 µm long-wave infrared.
  4. Calibration certificate: Traceable to NIST standards.
  5. FDA listing: Registered as a telethermographic device under 21 CFR 884.2980.

Legitimate vendors carry FDA device registration; operators should hold CCT or equivalent clinical thermography certification.

  • FDA device listing: Telethermographic systems are listed under 21 CFR 884.2980 (accessdata.fda.gov).

Myths vs Facts About Medical Thermography

Myth: Thermography replaces mammography.

Learn more: Med Hot vs FLIR Medical Thermography: 2026 Comparison

Fact: The FDA states thermography is adjunctive only and must not replace mammography for breast cancer screening.

Myth: Any infrared camera works for clinical use.

Fact: Clinical work requires NETD under 0.05°C and FDA-listed devices.

Myth: Software is optional — you can just save JPEGs.

Fact: Radiometric data is lost in JPEGs; clinical software preserves per-pixel temperature for valid comparisons.

Myth: Thermography reports can be read by any clinician.

Fact: Interpretation requires specific training in thermal pattern analysis.

#Red flags to watch for

  • Vendor cannot produce an FDA device listing number.
  • Claims that thermography "diagnoses" cancer or any condition as a standalone tool.
  • No Business Associate Agreement offered for PHI handling.
  • Software stores only JPEGs without underlying radiometric data.
  • No interpretation network or unclear interpreter credentials.
  • Demands full payment upfront with no demo or trial period.
