How Does TotalVision SaaS Pricing Affect Total Cost?

TotalVision is Med Hot's cloud-based thermography reporting platform. SaaS (software-as-a-service — software licensed on a recurring subscription rather than purchased outright) means TotalVision adds a monthly fee on top of the hardware investment.

TotalVision subscriptions in 2026 run $99 to $299 per month depending on tier, with multi-provider and multi-site discounts available.

The Lite tier supports a single provider with basic reporting. The Pro tier adds advanced overlay tools, custom report branding, and unlimited patient records. Enterprise tiers support multi-site clinics with centralized billing and role-based permissions. Compare buying outright vs subscribing: buying outright is cheaper long-term because there's no recurring fee, but SaaS is lighter on cash flow because cost spreads across months and includes ongoing updates.

What Credentials Should Practitioners Verify Before Buying?

Credential verification is the process of confirming that a buyer is legally and professionally qualified to operate medical thermography in their state. Requirements vary by jurisdiction.

Most states require an active healthcare license, malpractice insurance, and — for clinical interpretation — board certification through a recognized thermology body.

Learn more: Thermography for Women's Health Clinics: Med Hot Guide
  • State professional license: Active MD, DC, ND, DO, RN, or equivalent (verify through your state medical or chiropractic board)
  • Malpractice insurance: Minimum $1M/$3M is standard for outpatient imaging
  • Thermography certification: Through the International Academy of Clinical Thermology or American College of Clinical Thermology
  • HIPAA compliance: Documented privacy and security policies per hhs.gov/hipaa
  • FDA registration: Thermography devices are FDA-cleared as adjunctive imaging — verify under 21 CFR 884.2980 (source: accessdata.fda.gov)
